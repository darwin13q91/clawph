The 12-Point Checklist

Amazon Seller's
Pre-Launch Checklist

The critical checkpoints that separate sellers who launch to crickets from sellers who hit the ground running

Most Amazon sellers skip 3-4 of these steps. Then they wonder why their product ranks on page 5, gets no reviews, and bleeds money on PPC. This checklist isn't theory — it's the exact process used by 7-figure sellers to launch products that convert from day one.

01

Validate Demand Beyond "It Looks Good"

Don't trust your gut. Use tools like Helium 10 or Jungle Scout to verify monthly search volume (aim for 3K+), check sales velocity, and analyze the revenue distribution among top sellers.

Red Flag: If the top 3 sellers own 80%+ of the market, it's likely too concentrated.
02

Calculate True Profit Margins (Not Just "Seems Good")

Include ALL costs: COGS, FBA fees, shipping to Amazon, PPC (15-30% of revenue), returns (5-10%), and storage fees. Target 25%+ net margin after everything.

Use the formula: Sale Price - (COGS + FBA + PPC + Returns + Storage) = True Profit
03

Analyze Reviews for Weaknesses

Read 50+ reviews of your top 5 competitors. Look for recurring complaints — these are product improvements waiting to happen. Also note what's praised; that's your baseline.

Pro Move: Create a spreadsheet categorizing complaints by frequency. Address the top 3 in your product design.
04

Verify Your Differentiation Is Real

"Better quality" isn't differentiation. "Comes with X accessory" is. Your unique angle must be visible in the main image and stated in the title.

Test It: Can someone understand why they should buy yours in 3 seconds? If not, sharpen your angle.
05

Design Images for Mobile-First Shopping

60%+ of Amazon shopping happens on mobile. Your main image needs to communicate value at thumbnail size. Lifestyle images should show the product IN USE, not just floating on white.

Checklist: Main image shows product in context? Text readable on phone? 7 images total with infographics?
06

Write Bullets That Sell Benefits, Not Features

Don't say "Made of 100% cotton." Say "Sleep cooler with breathable 100% cotton that wicks away moisture." Lead with the outcome, support with the feature.

Formula: [Benefit] + [Feature] + [Proof/Result]. Apply to all 5 bullets.
07

Master Backend Keywords (Most Sellers Skip This)

Use all 249 bytes in your backend search terms. Include synonyms, misspellings, Spanish terms, and related uses. Don't repeat words from your title or bullets — it's wasted space.

Hidden Gem: Use spaces, not commas, to maximize character usage. "water bottle sport" > "water,bottle,sport"
08

Plan Your Launch Budget (PPC Isn't Optional)

You need 50-100 units for giveaways/rebates and $500-1000 for PPC in month one. If you can't afford this, delay launch. A product without momentum dies.

Budget Rule: Have 2x your COGS available in cash for launch activities. This covers giveaways, PPC, and initial ad spend.
09

Set Up Your Review Generation System

You need 10-15 reviews for social proof. Use the Request a Review button manually, or better, set up automated follow-up sequences. Have a plan for getting those first critical reviews.

Ethical Hack: Launch at a slight discount to drive volume faster. Volume = more review opportunities.
10

Check for IP/Hazmat/Restricted Issues

Before you order inventory, verify your product isn't restricted, hazmat, or IP-protected. One surprise here can kill your entire investment.

Verify: Search Amazon's restricted products list, check for patents/trademarks, and confirm your supplier has the right certifications.
11

Create Your Brand Story (A+ Content)

If you're Brand Registered, A+ Content can increase conversion by 5-10%. Tell your brand story, show comparison charts, and add lifestyle imagery. Do this BEFORE launch.

Structure: Brand Story (emotional) → Product Comparison (rational) → Lifestyle Images (aspirational).
12

Build Your Post-Launch Monitoring System

Set up alerts for Buy Box loss, negative reviews, and inventory levels. Know your key metrics (conversion rate, ACOS, sessions) and check them weekly minimum.

Automation: Use tools like Helium 10 Alerts or build custom notifications. The faster you catch issues, the faster you fix them.
